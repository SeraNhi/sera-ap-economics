Economics Overview

Building a strong foundation before diving into AP Economics.

What is Economics?

Economics is the study of how individuals, businesses, and governments allocate scarce resources to satisfy unlimited wants and needs. It examines decision-making, incentives, markets, production, consumption, and the trade-offs people face every day.

At its core, economics seeks to answer three fundamental questions:

  • What should be produced?
  • How should it be produced?
  • For whom should it be produced?

Why Study Economics?

Economics provides powerful analytical tools that help explain how societies function and how decisions affect individuals and markets.

Understanding economics improves critical thinking and allows students to interpret financial news, public policy, business decisions, and global economic events.

Economic reasoning is valuable in careers such as finance, business, law, government, public policy, consulting, entrepreneurship, and data analysis.

AP Macroeconomics Overview

AP Macroeconomics studies the economy as a whole. Students learn how national economies grow, experience inflation, create jobs, and interact with other nations.

Major topics include GDP, unemployment, inflation, aggregate demand, aggregate supply, fiscal policy, monetary policy, economic growth, and international trade.

Macroeconomics helps explain economic performance at the national and global levels.

AP Microeconomics Overview

AP Microeconomics focuses on individual decision makers, firms, and specific markets.

Students explore how consumers maximize utility, how businesses determine output and pricing, and how different market structures affect efficiency and competition.

Topics include supply and demand, elasticity, production costs, monopoly, competition, labor markets, externalities, and market failure.
